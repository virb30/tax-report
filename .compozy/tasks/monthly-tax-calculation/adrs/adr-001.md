# ADR-001: Define V1 Scope for Monthly Tax Calculation

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-07

## Context

Tax Report currently supports import, position recalculation, and annual assets reporting, but it does not expose a
first-class monthly tax calculation workflow. The user wants to make monthly tax due a core product capability for
Brazilian individual investors based on recorded transactions, with a monthly consolidated experience rather than a
post-import estimate.

The requested scope includes common cash-market operations in stocks, FIIs, ETFs, and units; weighted-average cost;
monthly tax outcomes; loss carryforward; IRRF handling; and more precise monetary storage. The current codebase
already uses high-precision decimal arithmetic internally and stores key monetary values as text, but the fee allocator
still rounds operational costs to BRL cents and the asset model does not distinguish units from stocks.

Research showed that Receita Federal's ReVar teaches users a monthly tax workflow with month-level summaries, carried
losses, and payment-oriented outcomes. Official sources also confirm monthly calculation and the BRL 20,000 exemption
for stock sales in the cash market. However, this research pass did not find an official Receita source that
unequivocally confirms the exemption treatment for units, which creates a trust and policy risk for V1.

## Decision

V1 will be a first-class monthly tax calculation feature for regular cash-market operations in stocks, FIIs, ETFs, and
units. It will prioritize trust and auditability over breadth.

V1 will include:

- A monthly consolidated screen as a primary product surface.
- Month outcome states: `no tax`, `exempt`, and `DARF due`.
- Audit memory per month with sales volume, realized gain/loss, exemption usage, carried losses, IRRF, tax base, and
  final tax due.
- Rule-aware grouping across stocks, FIIs, ETFs, and units, with the BRL 20,000 exemption applied only to qualifying
  stock sales in V1.
- Six-decimal persistence and calculation fidelity for transaction prices, average cost, and fee apportionment.
- Explicit tax-rule distinction for units in V1, derived only from assets whose base class is `stock` and whose ticker
  ends with `11`, preventing confusion with FIIs that also commonly end with `11`.
- Conservative treatment of FIIs, ETFs, and units as non-exempt in V1, with visible disclosure in the product and
  audit trail where interpretation needs to be made explicit.
- A minimal persisted monthly calculation artifact with calculation version and the facts needed to preserve historical
  auditability across reimports and rule changes.

V1 will not require a heavyweight persisted ledger of every intermediate line if deterministic recalculation from
canonical transactions remains rigorous and reproducible. The persisted month artifact is the minimum acceptable audit
boundary.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Monthly Estimate Without First-Class Tax State

- **Description**: Add a monthly tax summary to the UI, but derive results on demand without explicit monthly audit
  outputs, unit separation, or stronger persistence.
- **Pros**: Faster to ship, fewer schema changes, lower upfront modeling cost.
- **Cons**: Weak auditability, high trust risk, silent drift from rounding and reclassification, poor fit for a core
  tax promise.
- **Why rejected**: It would look complete in the UI while remaining structurally too fragile for a flagship tax
  feature.

### Alternative 2: Broad Tax Platform in V1

- **Description**: Ship monthly tax calculation plus DARF workflow automation, annual tax integration, day trade, and
  broader asset/regime coverage.
- **Pros**: Stronger headline feature set, closer parity with mature category tools.
- **Cons**: Large scope, higher legal and modeling risk, slower path to a trustworthy core workflow.
- **Why rejected**: It expands breadth before the core monthly trust loop is proven and would dilute effort away from
  correctness.

### Alternative 3: Exclude Units and ETFs Until Legal Validation Is Stronger

- **Description**: Support only stocks and FIIs in authoritative monthly output, leaving ETFs and units out of final
  tax calculation until rule treatment is validated more strongly.
- **Pros**: Lower legal ambiguity, narrower risk surface.
- **Cons**: Weakens product completeness for the target user, creates an obvious gap in a mainstream retail workflow.
- **Why rejected**: The user explicitly wants ETFs and units covered and the market expectation is a complete monthly
  close for common assets; exclusion reduces product value too much if conservative treatment and disclosure can
  contain the risk.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The product moves from position tracking into a monetizable monthly tax workflow across the main mainstream asset
  groups used by the target user.
- Historical recalculation becomes explicit and understandable through month obsolescence and targeted recomputation
  flows.
- The architecture remains narrow in market breadth while strong in accounting correctness.
- Users get a trustable monthly close with visible reasoning, not a black-box number.
- The system gains a cleaner path for future tax-rule evolution through explicit unit classification and calculation
  versioning.

### Negative

- V1 requires meaningful model and persistence changes before any UI win is visible.
- Unit handling remains a policy-sensitive area and needs careful disclosure.
- Persisting month-level artifacts adds some complexity even without a full ledger.

### Risks

- **Legal-policy risk for units**: conservative non-exempt treatment may later need revision.
  Mitigation: make the rule explicit in product copy, audit output, and future rule versioning.
- **Precision drift risk**: fee allocation and average-cost paths may still round too early.
  Mitigation: keep six-decimal fidelity until the monthly output boundary and add regression tests around edge months.
- **Historical consistency risk**: reimports or reclassification changes may alter prior months.
  Mitigation: persist a minimal monthly artifact with calculation version, classification basis, and final outcome.

## Implementation Notes

- Distinguish unit treatment from ordinary stocks in rule evaluation, deriving units in V1 from stock-class tickers that
  end with `11`.
- Rework fee apportionment so calculation-grade precision is preserved before currency rounding in presentation or
  settlement outputs.
- Treat the monthly feature as a dedicated product area via a new primary tab adjacent to positions and annual
  reporting.
- Preserve deterministic recalculation from canonical transactions, but persist enough month-level facts to explain and
  stabilize historical outputs. The minimum monthly artifact should capture month, calculation version, policy flags,
  opening carried losses, closing carried losses, grouped sales totals, grouped realized gain/loss, IRRF consumed, and
  final outcome.
- When underlying inputs change, mark affected months as obsolete instead of silently rewriting trust-critical outputs.
  Provide both per-month recalculation and full recalculation actions.
