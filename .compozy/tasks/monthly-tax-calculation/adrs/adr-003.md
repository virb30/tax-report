# ADR-003: Use Fixed Monthly Tax Groups and Preserve Below-Minimum Roll-Forward

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-07

## Context

During PRD review, the user clarified two product-level decisions that shape how month outcomes should be presented and
understood.

First, when the tax due for a month is below BRL 10.00, the month should not be flattened into a generic tax-due
state. Instead, the month should visibly indicate that the amount is below the collection threshold and that the value
must be carried into subsequent months.

Second, the user wants the month explanation grouped into three explicit business-facing categories:

- `Geral - Comum` for taxable non-exempt operations.
- `Geral - Isento` for exempt stock results.
- `FII` for FII-specific results.

The user also clarified that the detailed view should focus on the amounts due under the applicable rate, rather than
turning the month detail into a broader tax-education surface.

These choices tighten the user-facing product model and remove ambiguity from how month results are communicated.

## Decision

The MVP will present supported monthly results using a fixed grouping model and an explicit below-minimum payable
state.

This means:

- A month may display a distinct `below R$ 10.00` indicator when the payable amount is under the minimum collection
  threshold.
- That below-minimum amount is carried forward into later months rather than treated as a settled end state.
- Month summaries and explanations use the following categories:
  - `Geral - Comum`
  - `Geral - Isento`
  - `FII`
- The month detail emphasizes calculated due amounts by category and applicable rate, rather than broad narrative
  explanations beyond what is required for user trust and verification.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Keep Below-Minimum Amounts Inside Generic Tax-Due Output

- **Description**: Show months below BRL 10.00 as ordinary tax-due months and explain the carry-forward only in
  secondary detail.
- **Pros**: Simpler state model, fewer distinct labels.
- **Cons**: Hides an important legal and user-facing distinction, weakens month status clarity.
- **Why rejected**: The user explicitly wants a visible state for this scenario.

### Alternative 2: Use Flexible or Asset-By-Asset Category Labels

- **Description**: Let the interface derive categories more loosely from asset classes or show each asset type
  separately.
- **Pros**: More extensible for future breadth, closer to an internal calculation model.
- **Cons**: Harder to scan, more complex for the user, weaker consistency across months.
- **Why rejected**: The user chose a stable, explicit three-category view for V1.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The month result becomes easier to scan and compare across history.
- Users can distinguish true payable months from below-threshold carry-forward months immediately.
- The product gets a stable presentation model for V1 that supports auditability without excessive verbosity.

### Negative

- The fixed grouping model may need revision when broader asset scope is added.
- Some internal calculation nuance may be compressed in the user-facing presentation.

### Risks

- **Interpretation risk**: users may assume the three groups are exhaustive for all future tax scenarios.
  Mitigation: describe the grouping as the supported V1 view for the current scope.
- **Expansion risk**: later categories may not fit cleanly into the initial display model.
  Mitigation: keep the PRD explicit that the grouping is tied to V1 scope and may expand in later phases.

## Implementation Notes

- The below-minimum state should remain part of the month lifecycle and history, not only a detail line.
- Carry-forward behavior should be visible enough that users understand why a later month includes prior unpaid
  amounts.
- The detail view should remain concise and calculation-oriented, emphasizing the due values within the approved
  categories.
