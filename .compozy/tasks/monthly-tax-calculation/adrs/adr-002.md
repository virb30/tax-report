# ADR-002: Choose an Audit-First History Workspace for Monthly Tax Close

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-07

## Context

The PRD clarification round established that the MVP should optimize for a single retail investor who wants to trust
the monthly tax result across the full imported history, not just the latest month. The user explicitly prioritized
auditability over speed when trade-offs are required.

The same clarification round also established that the MVP should:

- Support rebuilding and reviewing the full imported history as a primary journey.
- Allow manual completion of missing facts such as costs, IRRF, or classifications before recalculation.
- Block months when confidence is too low, while still giving the user a guided way to resolve the missing facts.
- Treat a successful month close as the user seeing the final result with a clear audit trail, without requiring
  payment workflow automation in V1.

Research reinforced that market expectations are month-centric and audit-sensitive. Receita Federal's ReVar presents
monthly cards, month detail, exemption analysis, carried losses, and payment outcomes, while commercial products such
as Mycapital and Grana compete on monthly apuracao and DARF readiness. Within this landscape, the clearest product
position for Tax Report is a monthly close the user can verify, not merely a fast summary.

## Decision

The MVP will use an audit-first history workspace as its primary product approach.

This means:

- The main journey starts from the user's imported month history rather than only the latest unresolved month.
- Each month has an explicit state such as closed, blocked, obsolete, or needs review.
- Opening a month reveals the explanation behind the result, including grouped outcomes and the facts that produced the
  result.
- If the month cannot be closed confidently, the product blocks closure, identifies what is missing, and allows the
  user to add the missing facts and recalculate.
- The MVP centers on explanation and trust, not on downstream payment workflow management.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Current-Month Closer with Historical Backfill

- **Description**: Optimize the experience around the latest unresolved month and treat older months as secondary.
- **Pros**: Faster recurring workflow, simpler home state, lighter first-run experience.
- **Cons**: Weak fit for full-history reconstruction, weaker audit narrative, less aligned with the chosen trust
  priority.
- **Why rejected**: The user explicitly wants full imported history to be part of the main journey and chose
  auditability as the deciding priority.

### Alternative 2: Compliance Companion with Follow-Through

- **Description**: Expand the monthly workflow to include stronger payment-oriented states, reminders, and follow-up
  beyond the calculation itself.
- **Pros**: Stronger obligation-prevention story, closer to a complete compliance loop.
- **Cons**: Pulls the MVP into payment and post-calculation workflow before the core explanation layer is proven.
- **Why rejected**: The user defined success as a clear month result with audit trail, not in-product payment
  completion or obligation management.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The monthly feature gets a clear and differentiated product identity based on trust and verification.
- Full-history users can adopt the product without first flattening their workflow into an external spreadsheet.
- Month states create a clean mental model for review, repair, recalculation, and confidence.
- The PRD can keep the MVP narrow by excluding payment workflow while still solving the main user anxiety.

### Negative

- The first-run experience may feel heavier than a latest-month-only workflow.
- The product must explain blocked months well or users may perceive the feature as cumbersome.
- The history-first approach raises expectations for stable recalculation and visible month state changes.

### Risks

- **Review fatigue risk**: users with long histories may feel overwhelmed.
  Mitigation: emphasize state-based triage and surface the next blocked or unresolved month clearly.
- **Perceived complexity risk**: detailed month explanations may feel dense.
  Mitigation: keep the top-level result simple and progressive, with deeper detail available on demand.
- **Scope creep risk**: blocked-month repair could drift into broad manual bookkeeping.
  Mitigation: limit manual inputs in V1 to the facts required to close supported months confidently.

## Implementation Notes

- The primary workspace should present months as the organizing unit of the experience.
- Month detail should separate final outcome, explanation, and missing-facts repair actions clearly.
- Manual intervention in V1 should focus on completing missing facts, not overriding the final result directly.
- Payment workflow, reminders, and obligation follow-through remain outside the core MVP promise unless later ADRs
  change that direction.
