# ADR-001: Adopt an End-to-End Correction MVP for Bens e Direitos

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-04-28

## Context

The product currently generates an annual Bens e Direitos report, but the workflow is not trustworthy enough for a self-service investor who expects declaration-ready output. Asset type can fall back incorrectly, report items are still shaped by broker allocation instead of declaration item, issuer metadata is not operationally maintainable, initial balance is not editable as a document, and unsupported cases are not surfaced as first-class pending work.

The user chose to optimize first for trustworthy declaration-ready output for a simple individual investor, while still keeping the MVP boundary broad enough to correct the full workflow rather than shipping a narrow report-only patch.

## Decision

The MVP will cover the full end-to-end correction flow for Bens e Direitos. It will:

- Resolve or review asset type before data is treated as ready.
- Support operational completion of issuer metadata required by the annual report.
- Surface pending issues clearly and block copy for items that are not ready.
- Provide historical correction for legacy asset typing problems.
- Redesign the annual report around declaration items instead of broker-level copy rows.
- Redesign initial balance as an editable multi-broker document.
- Continue processing supported data when unsupported lines are found, while routing unsupported lines into visible pending work.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Report-Centric MVP

- **Description**: Fix the annual report and only the minimum supporting flows needed to improve output quality.
- **Pros**: Smaller scope, faster delivery, lower coordination cost.
- **Cons**: Leaves bad historical data and fragile starting balances in place; risks a report that still depends on manual cleanup outside the product.
- **Why rejected**: It improves presentation faster than it improves trust, which conflicts with the chosen goal of declaration-ready output.

### Alternative 2: Data-Remediation MVP

- **Description**: Prioritize catalog cleanup, legacy repair, and unsupported-case handling before reworking the report experience.
- **Pros**: Strong long-term data foundation; reduces hidden inconsistencies.
- **Cons**: Delays the user-visible annual declaration outcome that gives this work immediate value.
- **Why rejected**: It postpones the main user payoff and does not center the annual declaration workflow strongly enough.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The MVP will feel coherent to end users instead of like several disconnected fixes.
- The product can present a stronger claim around declaration readiness for supported cases.
- Historical inconsistencies can be repaired inside the app rather than outside it.

### Negative

- Scope is larger than a report-only release.
- More product surfaces must remain consistent in one rollout: import, catalog, report, and initial balance.

### Risks

- Broader scope can slip if edge cases expand beyond MVP intent.
- Users may still expect support for more asset classes or events than the product can safely handle.
- Mitigation: keep supported scope explicit, mark pending work clearly, and defer non-essential classes and events to later phases.

## Implementation Notes

This ADR is product-scoping guidance for the PRD. Detailed solution design belongs in the TechSpec stage.
