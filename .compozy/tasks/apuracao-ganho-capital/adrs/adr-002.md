# ADR-002: Annual Month-by-Month Assessment as V1 Product Approach

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-04

## Context

The V1 feature must help Brazilian individual investors use existing portfolio data to
prepare annual IRPF Renda Variavel values. The user selected annual copy support as the
main V1 outcome, with a month-by-month annual table as the primary copy aid.

The V1 scope is limited to supported B3 spot-market stocks, FIIs, and ETFs. Day trade
operations should be marked unsupported. For supported operations, same-category loss
compensation should be applied inside the assessed annual view so users can understand
taxable, exempt, compensated, and loss-carrying monthly results.

## Decision

Use an annual month-by-month assessment approach for V1.

The product will present a selected tax year as monthly Renda Variavel rows derived from
supported source transactions. Each month should show status, supported asset category,
taxable gains, exempt stock gains, losses, same-category compensation, remaining losses,
and blockers. The annual view should be optimized for manual IRPF entry while preserving
enough monthly detail for review.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Compact Annual Summary

- **Description**: Prioritize annual totals, with monthly detail available only as
  supporting evidence.
- **Pros**: Faster user workflow; simpler first screen; easier to scan for users who only
  need declaration totals.
- **Cons**: Weaker reviewability; hides the month-by-month structure required by Receita's
  Renda Variavel flow; makes blockers harder to localize.
- **Why rejected**: The user chose month-by-month annual output as the main copy aid.

### Alternative 2: Assessment Ledger

- **Description**: Prioritize a detailed audit ledger for every supported sale, deriving
  monthly and annual summaries from that ledger.
- **Pros**: Strongest traceability; useful for power users and accountant review.
- **Cons**: Heavier UX; more detail than the MVP needs; risks delaying the annual IRPF
  copy-support outcome.
- **Why rejected**: The user wants annual copy support with monthly rows, not a transaction
  ledger as the primary product surface.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Aligns the V1 experience with Receita's month-by-month Renda Variavel declaration flow.
- Gives users annual copy support without hiding the monthly basis of each value.
- Keeps V1 focused on stocks, FIIs, and ETFs while making unsupported day trade explicit.
- Supports same-category loss compensation in the view users rely on for annual IRPF.

### Negative

- V1 will not offer the shortest possible annual-only workflow.
- Users who need transaction-level audit detail may need to inspect traces rather than use
  the main table alone.
- The feature must explain monthly statuses clearly enough for annual users who do not
  think in tax-month terms.

### Risks

- **User confusion**: Mitigate with clear month status labels, annual totals, and visible
  blockers.
- **Scope pressure**: Mitigate by keeping day trade, DARF generation, BDRs, derivatives,
  and official filing out of V1.
- **False confidence**: Mitigate by preventing unsupported or pending months from being
  treated as ready annual evidence.

## Implementation Notes

- This ADR defines product scope only. Technical design belongs in the later TechSpec.
- Preserve the additive direction accepted in ADR-001.
- Use the existing idea document and current PRD process as references for V1 scope.
