# ADR-001: Additive Monthly Assessment Before DARF Engine

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-04

## Context

The product needs to support Brazilian individual investors who must assess B3
Renda Variavel capital gains and losses month by month. The core user need is not only
annual declaration formatting; users must understand taxable gains, deductible losses,
exempt stock-sale months under the R$ 20,000 monthly sales threshold, and results that are
pending or unsupported because source data is incomplete.

The existing application already has import, positions, asset catalog, fee allocation,
average price, and Bens e Direitos annual reporting workflows. Those workflows must remain
unchanged for this feature. A full DARF engine would require broader support for IRRF,
paid tax memory, prior-year loss carryforward, and more edge cases than V1 should carry.

## Decision

Create the V1 feature as a separate, additive monthly capital gains/loss assessment
workflow for supported B3 spot-market stocks, FIIs, and ETFs.

The assessment should consume existing transaction, fee, asset, and position data as
source facts, produce status-driven monthly results, and consolidate those assessed
results into annual IRPF evidence. Existing import, positions, asset catalog, and Bens e
Direitos behavior must not be modified by V1.

DARF generation, payment workflow, full paid-tax memory, automated B3/ReVar integration,
and broader unsupported asset classes are deferred.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Annual-Only Declaration Formatting

- **Description**: Produce annual IRPF fields directly from existing portfolio data without
  introducing a monthly assessment model.
- **Pros**: Faster initial delivery; smaller UI and contract surface.
- **Cons**: Misrepresents the monthly nature of Receita's Renda Variavel flow; increases
  later rework for DARF, loss tracking, and exemption handling.
- **Why rejected**: The feature's central value is apuração of gains and losses, including
  exempt monthly results, not just annual formatting.

### Alternative 2: Full Monthly DARF Engine in V1

- **Description**: Implement monthly tax due calculation, DARF generation, IRRF treatment,
  paid tax memory, and loss carryforward from the start.
- **Pros**: More complete user outcome; closer to established competitors.
- **Cons**: Larger rule surface, higher correctness risk, greater security/privacy
  exposure, and slower validation of the core assessment workflow.
- **Why rejected**: V1 should prove trustworthy monthly assessment before taking on the
  operational and liability burden of DARF issuance.

### Alternative 3: Modify Existing Bens e Direitos Report

- **Description**: Fold Renda Variavel results into the existing annual holdings report.
- **Pros**: Reuses an existing annual report surface.
- **Cons**: Couples holdings declaration behavior to realized capital gains assessment;
  risks regressions in an existing workflow the user explicitly wants preserved.
- **Why rejected**: The feature must be additive and must not modify existing behavior.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Preserves all existing product behavior while adding a new tax assessment capability.
- Models Receita's monthly Renda Variavel structure early enough to support future DARF
  expansion.
- Enables audit-first output with ready, pending, and unsupported statuses.
- Keeps asset-specific rules explicit so FIIs and ETFs do not inherit stock exemption
  behavior.

### Negative

- V1 will not generate DARF or fully replace monthly tax tooling.
- Users may still need external tools for unsupported operations, day trade, derivatives,
  foreign assets, or paid-tax memory.
- The annual declaration summary depends on the completeness and trustworthiness of the
  monthly assessment.

### Risks

- **False confidence**: Mitigate by blocking ready output when data is incomplete,
  ambiguous, or unsupported.
- **Rule drift**: Mitigate by isolating rule constants and adding rule-focused tests around
  Receita thresholds and asset-specific treatment.
- **Regression risk**: Mitigate by keeping the feature additive and running existing import,
  position, catalog, and Bens e Direitos tests.
- **Sensitive financial data exposure**: Mitigate by keeping IPC typed, validating imports,
  and treating future B3/ReVar integrations as a separate security decision.

## Implementation Notes

- Add a separate Renda Variavel assessment surface instead of changing the current Bens e
  Direitos annual report behavior.
- Use existing transaction, fee, asset, and position data only as source facts.
- Treat derivatives, options, futures, term market, securities lending, gold financial
  asset, foreign assets, and automated B3/ReVar integration as out of scope for V1.
- Keep DARF generation and payment workflows out of V1 while preserving domain concepts
  needed for later expansion.
